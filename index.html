November 1st week - Halloween released!

07 Nov 2022

GENERIC RPG WEEKLY REPORT

I released the halloween update. I didn’t advertise the event because I’m trying to focus more on long-term goals (getting the new GRI update going and start planning the next update) than short-term ones (making extra money). I’m thinking there might be only 1~3 updates until launch. I mean 1 update => final update for release.

Next update:

  • Sounds and music, new animations and effects
  • MAYBE: more unknown tab content

The polish criteria is still this:

  • How easily reusable is this improvement for other projects?
  • How fast can I make it?
  • Do I have a good reference to use when making the polish?
  • Does the polish look good on trailers?
  • Does the polish make the game easier to understand?
    • makes it easier for people who just started to understand the game
    • visualized that actions happened
    • how actions actually work
  • How often will new players see this polish?
  • how often will long-term players see this polish?
  • Is the polish making the game prettier but less enjoyable to play somehow?

But I found a sound guy and want to get it done. So I don’t think I’ll be adding any additional effects other than what I have planned. Might enhance what is already there though. Maybe that’s more of sequel material though. _____________________

October 2nd week - Remaking the UI V

10 Oct 2022

GENERIC RPG WEEKLY REPORT

I released a small beta for the changes. I got some nice feedback and made some adjustments. Things SEEM fine. No bugs coming up yet.

Next update: A) Equipment screen update B) Battle screen update C) Chapter 2 D) QOL

The boost from 10 reviews was dying but then I posted the game on feedback friday asking for feedback on the beta. Some folks ignored the beta instructions part and just bought the game straight away. I think the (positive) label is also improving quality of when I post the game for people to see. People know the game isn’t broken because of the positive reviews and all. This is just a theory, but since conversion rate is probably increased, every person I send to Steam is a high quality visit which makes Steam want to promote the page a little bit. And since conversion rate is also up when Steam promotes the page, the boost is bigger. What I want to say is that the 10 reviews thing really creates a cummulative positive effect for everything you do Steam related. _____________________
